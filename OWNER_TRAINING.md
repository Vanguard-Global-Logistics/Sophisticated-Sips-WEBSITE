# Owner Training Guide — for Amy
Plain English, no jargon. Read once, then keep for reference. The same content lives inside your dashboard at /owner/training.

## Your daily routine (5–10 minutes, coffee in hand)
1. Open your site + /owner on your phone. 2. Tap **✦ Ask AI** — read your briefing. 3. Open the **Approval Queue** — approve or decline any waiting emails. 4. Glance at the **Pipeline** for new leads (they arrive automatically from the website and concierge). 5. If today has events, the briefing already told you the supplies. Done.

## Reviewing bookings
Pipeline tab. Each row shows who, what kind of event, when, how many guests, and a score (higher = more valuable). Tap **Contacted** after you've spoken with them, **Confirm** when the date is locked (this creates the event and deposit), **Decline** if it's dead, **📞 Call** to phone them, **✦ Draft outreach** to have an email written for your approval.

## Getting paid (Square)
Payments tab, on the confirmed event: **Deposit link ($X)** → the link is copied automatically → paste it into a text to your client. When the deposit shows paid, **Send balance invoice** emails them a proper Square invoice for the rest. **Full payment** exists for clients who prefer to pay everything at once. Stuck payment? **Check payment** asks Square directly. Refunds: do those inside your Square app/dashboard.

## Approving AI emails
Approval Queue. Read every draft like you wrote it — because to the customer, you did. Approve & send, or Decline. The system adds your business address and an unsubscribe link automatically, caps follow-ups at three, and permanently blocks anyone who opts out.

## Concierge leads
Leads marked **concierge** gave their details to your website's AI assistant and agreed to be contacted — they're warm. Call or draft outreach same-day if you can.

## Lead Finder (the safe way)
Found a public event announcement — chamber calendar, school newsletter, festival vendor call? Copy the text, paste it into Lead Finder, tap Extract. Rules that keep you safe: only genuinely public listings, only contact emails actually printed in them, and every email still goes through your Approval Queue.

## Adding menu items
Menu Editor tab → pick a category → name, price, one tasty sentence → **Add to menu**. It's live on the public site immediately. **Hide** takes an item off the site without deleting it (perfect for seasonal drinks).

## Reading your numbers
Insights tab. The gold bars are real Square revenue by month. "Lead → confirmed conversion" tells you how many inquiries become bookings. "Where demand comes from" tells you what to market more of. Profit estimate on the Daily Summary assumes ~45% costs until we tune it to your real numbers.

## Unsubscribes
Nothing to do — the system handles it and will refuse to contact them again. Just never email them from your personal account either.

## Troubleshooting payments
Paid but shows pending → **Check payment**. Link won't create → the Setup Wizard's Square step shows connection status. Anything else → screenshot the message and the time it happened.

## Getting technical help
Screenshot: (1) what you saw, (2) the time. The developer checks Vercel → Logs for that minute; between your screenshot and those logs, nearly anything is fixable quickly. Password reset, if ever needed, is in Supabase → Authentication → Users.
